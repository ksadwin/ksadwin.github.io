Linguistic Self-Expression on Dating Websites

An in-progress, undergraduate research project with Beth Dellea that seeks to identify idiosyncratic features of language on the dating website OkCupid. Over 1500 user profiles have been scraped and tagged with gender, sexual orientation, location, and age as reported by the user on the website. The full text of profiles and their tags were stored in a SQLite database. Ongoing analysis includes measuring linguistic features using Natural Language Processing (NLP) and using K means clustering on the corpus of profiles. The most concise and engaging results appear when the profiles are visualized using word clouds grouped by the tags.

Findings to be presented at the New York Celebration of Women in Computing (NYCWiC) and the James J. Whalen Academic Symposium in Spring 2016.

  • Date: Fall 2015 (ongoing)
  • Platform: Python 3.4, SQLite 3

Gender Neutralizer

A Google Chrome extension that changes all he and she pronouns to they pronouns, and replaces gendered language. For instance, "her sister" becomes "their sibling". Began as an idea hatched at WiCHacks 2015 that never came to fruition... until now! Download it here. It's still pretty clunky (lots of "they is" and capitalization issues) but it's fun.

  • Date: WiCHacks 2015 (with edits Spring 2016)
  • Platform: HTML/CSS/JavaScript
